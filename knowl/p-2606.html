Let \(a, r \in \mathbb{R}\text{.}\) If the sequence \(S_1 ,S_2 , \ldots ,S_n , \ldots\) is defined by \(S_1 = a\) and for each \(n \in \mathbb{N}\text{,}\) \(S_{n + 1} = a + r \cdot S_n\text{,}\) then for each \(n \in \mathbb{N}\text{,}\) \(S_n = a + a \cdot r + a \cdot r^2 + \cdots + a \cdot r^{n - 1}\text{.}\) That is, the geometric series \(S_n\) is the sum of the first \(n\) terms of the corresponding geometric sequence.
