Definition.

Two functions \(f\) and \(g\) are equal provided that

  • The domain of \(f\) equals the domain of \(g\text{.}\) That is, \(\text{ dom} ( f ) = \text{ dom} ( g )\text{.}\)

  • The codomain of \(f\) equals the codomain of \(g\text{.}\) That is, \(\text{ codom} ( f ) = \text{ codom} ( g )\text{.}\)

  • For each \(x\) in the domain of \(f\) (which equals the domain of \(g\)), \(f( x ) = g( x )\text{.}\)
