Let \(a, b, c \in \Z_9\text{.}\) Since \(a^2 \equiv a^2 \pmod 9\text{,}\) we see that \(a \sim a\) and \(\sim\) is reflexive. Let \(a, b, c \in \Z_9\text{.}\) If \(a \sim b\text{,}\) then \(a^2 \equiv b^2 \pmod 9\) and hence, by the symmetric property of congruence, \(b^2 \equiv a^2 \pmod 9\text{.}\) This proves that \(\sim\) is symmetric. Finally, if \(a \sim b\) and \(b \sim c\text{,}\) then \(a^2 \equiv b^2 \pmod 9\) and \(b^2 \equiv c^2 \pmod 9\text{.}\) By the transitive property of congruence, we conclude that \(a^2 \equiv c^2 \pmod 9\) and hence, \(a \sim c\text{.}\) This proves that \(\sim\) is transitive. The distinct equivalence classes are \(\left\{0, 3, 6 \right\}\text{,}\) \(\left\{1, 8 \right\}\text{,}\) \(\left\{ 2, 7 \right\}\text{,}\) and \(\left\{ 4, 5 \right\}\text{.}\)
