Consequently, a natural number \(d\) is not the greatest common divisor of \(a\) and \(b\) provided that it does not satisfy at least one of these properties. That is, \(d\) is not equal to \(\gcd( {a, b} )\) provided that

  • \(d\) does not divide \(a\) or \(d\) does not divide \(b\text{;}\) or

  • There exists a natural number \(k\) such that \(k \mid a\) and \(k \mid b\) and \(k > d\text{.}\)
