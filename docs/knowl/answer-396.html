Answer 6.a.1.

Let \(x \in \left[ \dfrac{5}{7} \right]\text{.}\) Then \(x - \dfrac{5}{7} \in \Z\text{,}\) which means that there is an integer \(m\) such that \(x - \dfrac{5}{7} = m\text{,}\) or \(x = \dfrac{5}{7} + m\text{.}\) This proves that \(x \in \left\{ \left. m + \dfrac{5}{7} \right| m \in \Z \right\}\) and, hence, that \(\left[ \dfrac{5}{7} \right] \subseteq \left\{ \left. m + \dfrac{5}{7} \right| m \in \Z \right\}\text{.}\) We still need to prove that \(\left\{ \left. m + \dfrac{5}{7} \right| m \in \Z \right\} \subseteq \left[ \dfrac{5}{7} \right]\text{.}\)
