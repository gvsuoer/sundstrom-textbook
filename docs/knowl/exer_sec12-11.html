Exercise 11.

Let \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) and \(c\) be real numbers with \(a \ne 0\text{.}\) The solutions of the quadratic equation \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) are given by the quadratic formula, which states that the solutions are \(x_1\) and \(x_2\text{,}\) where

\begin{equation*} x_1 = \frac{-b + \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \text{ and } x_2 = \frac{-b - \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\text{.} \end{equation*}
(a)

Prove that the sum of the two solutions of the quadratic equation \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) is equal to \(- \dfrac{b}{a}\text{.}\)

(b)

Prove that the product of the two solutions of the quadratic equation \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) is equal to \(\dfrac{c}{a}\text{.}\)
