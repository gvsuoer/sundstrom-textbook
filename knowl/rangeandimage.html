Definition.

Let \(f\x A \to B\text{.}\) The set \(\left\{ {f( x ) \mid x \in A} \right\}\) is called the range of the function \(\boldsymbol{f}\) and is denoted by \(\text{ range} \left( f \right)\text{.}\) The range of \(f\) is sometimes called the image of the function \(\boldsymbol{f}\) (or the image of \(\boldsymbol{A}\) under \(\boldsymbol{f}\)).
