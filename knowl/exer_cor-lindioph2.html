Let \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) and \(c\) be integers with \(a \ne 0\) and \(b \ne 0\text{.}\) If \(a\) and \(b\) are relatively prime, then the linear Diophantine equation \(ax + by = c\) has infinitely many solutions. In addition, if \(\left( x_0, y_0 \right)\) is a particular solution of this equation, then all the solutions of the equation are given by
\begin{equation*} x = x_0 + b k \qquad y = y_0 - a k\!\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(k \in \mathbb{Z}\text{.}\)
