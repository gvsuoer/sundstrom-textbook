Let \(n \in \mathbb{N}\text{,}\) and let \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) and \(c\) be integers.

  1. For every integer \(a\text{,}\) \(a \equiv a \pmod n\text{.}\)

    This is called the reflexive property of congruence modulo \(n\text{.}\)

  2. If \(a \equiv b \pmod n\text{,}\) then \(b \equiv a \pmod n\text{.}\)

    This is called the symmetric property of congruence modulo \(n\text{.}\)

  3. If \(a \equiv b \pmod n\) and \(b \equiv c \pmod n\text{,}\) then \(a \equiv c \pmod n\text{.}\)

    This is called the transitive property of congruence modulo \(n\text{.}\)
