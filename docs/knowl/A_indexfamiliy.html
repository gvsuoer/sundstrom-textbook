Activity 33. An Indexed Family of Subsets of the Cartesian Plane.

Let \(\R^*\) be the set of nonnegative real numbers, and for each \(r \in \R^*\text{,}\) let

\begin{align*} C_r \amp = \left\{ (x, y) \in \R \times \R \mid x^2 + y^2 = r^2 \right\}\\ D_r \amp = \left\{ (x, y) \in \R \times \R \mid x^2 + y^2 \leq r^2 \right\}\\ T_r \amp = \left\{ (x, y) \in \R \times \R \mid x^2 + y^2 > r^2 \right\} = D_r^c\text{.} \end{align*}

If \(r > 0\text{,}\) then the set \(C_r\) is the circle of radius \(r\) with center at the origin as shown in Figure 5.43, and the set \(D_r\) is the shaded disk (including the boundary) shown in Figure 5.43.

(a)

Determine \(\bigcup\limits_{r \in \R^*}^{}C_r\) and \(\bigcap\limits_{r \in \R^*}^{}C_r\text{.}\)

(b)

Determine \(\bigcup\limits_{r \in \R^*}^{}D_r\) and \(\bigcap\limits_{r \in \R^*}^{}D_r\text{.}\)

(c)

Determine \(\bigcup\limits_{r \in \R^*}^{}T_r\) and \(\bigcap\limits_{r \in \R^*}^{}T_r\text{.}\)

(d)

Let \(\mathscr{C} = \left\{ C_r \mid r \in \R^* \right\}\text{,}\) \(\mathscr{D} = \left\{ D_r \mid r \in \R^* \right\}\text{,}\) and \(\mathscr{T} = \left\{ T_r \mid r \in \R^* \right\}\text{.}\) Are any of these indexed families of sets pairwise disjoint? Explain.

Now let \(I\) be the closed interval \([0, 2]\) and let \(J\) be the closed interval \([1, 2]\text{.}\)

(e)

Determine \(\bigcup\limits_{r \in I}^{}C_r\text{,}\) \(\bigcap\limits_{r \in I}^{}C_r\text{,}\) \(\bigcup\limits_{r \in J}^{}C_r\text{,}\) and \(\bigcap\limits_{r \in J}^{}C_r\text{.}\)

(f)

Determine \(\bigcup\limits_{r \in I}^{}D_r\text{,}\) \(\bigcap\limits_{r \in I}^{}D_r\text{,}\) \(\bigcup\limits_{r \in J}^{}D_r\text{,}\) and \(\bigcap\limits_{r \in J}^{}D_r\text{.}\)

(g)

Determine \(\left(\: \bigcup\limits_{r \in I}^{}D_r \right)^c\text{,}\) \(\left(\: \bigcap\limits_{r \in I}^{}D_r \right)^c\text{,}\) \(\left(\: \bigcup\limits_{r \in J}^{}D_r \right)^c\text{,}\) and \(\left(\: \bigcap\limits_{r \in J}^{}D_r \right)^c\text{.}\)

(h)

Determine \(\bigcup\limits_{r \in I}^{}T_r\text{,}\) \(\bigcap\limits_{r \in I}^{}T_r\text{,}\) \(\bigcup\limits_{r \in J}^{}T_r\text{,}\) and \(\bigcap\limits_{r \in J}^{}T_r\text{.}\)

(i)

Use De Morgan's Laws to explain the relationship between your answers in Task 33.g and Task 33.h.
