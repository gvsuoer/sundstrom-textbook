Item 3.

Item 2 was included to illustrate the fact that a rational number can be written as a fraction in “lowest terms” with a positive denominator. This means that any rational number can be written as a quotient \(\dfrac{m}{n}\text{,}\) where \(m\) and \(n\) are integers, \(n > 0\text{,}\) and \(m\) and \(n\) have no common factor greater than 1.

If \(n\) is an integer and \(n^2\) is even, what can be concluded about \(n\text{.}\) Refer to Theorem 3.8.
