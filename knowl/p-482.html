It is important to understand the use of the operator “or.” In mathematics, we use the “inclusive or” unless stated otherwise. This means that \(P \vee Q\) is true when both \(P\) and \(Q\) are true and also when only one of them is true. That is, \(P \vee Q\) is true when at least one of \(P\) or \(Q\) is true, or \(P \vee Q\) is false only when both \(P\) and \(Q\) are false.
