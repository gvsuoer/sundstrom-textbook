Activity 3. More Work with Pythagorean Triples.

In Activity 2, we verified that all of the following triples of natural numbers are Pythagorean triples:

  • 3, 4, and 5

  • 6, 8, and 10

  • 8, 15, and 17

  • 10, 24, and 26

  • 12, 35, and 37

  • 14, 48, and 50

(a)

Focus on the least even natural number in each of these Pythagorean triples. Let \(n\) be this even number and find \(m\) so that \(n = 2m\text{.}\) Now try to write formulas for the other two numbers in the Pythagorean triple in terms of \(m\text{.}\) For example, for 3, 4, and 5, \(n = 4\) and \(m = 2\text{.}\) Once you think you have formulas, test your results with \(m = 10\text{.}\) That is, check to see that you have a Pythagorean triple whose smallest even number is 20.
