Now compare the right side of the last equation to the right side of the equation in step \(Q1\text{.}\) Sometimes the difficult part at this point is the realization that \(q\) stands for some integer and that we only have to show that \(x \cdot y\) equals two times some integer plus one. Can we now make that conclusion? The answer is yes because we can factor a 2 from the first three terms on the right side of the equation and obtain

\begin{align*} x \cdot y \amp = 4mn + 2m + 2n + 1\\ \amp = 2 (2mn + m + n) + 1 \end{align*}
