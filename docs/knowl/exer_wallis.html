Exercise 16.

In calculus, it can be shown that

\begin{align*} \int_{}^{} \sin^{\,2} x \, dx \amp = \frac{x}{2} - \frac{1}{2} \sin x \cos x + c \text{ and }\\ \int_{}{} \cos^{\,2} x \, dx \amp = \frac{x}{2} + \frac{1}{2} \sin x \cos x + c\text{.} \end{align*}

Using integration by parts, it is also possible to prove that for each natural number \(n\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \int_{}{} \sin^{\,n} x \, dx \amp = -\frac{1}{n} \sin^{n-1}x \cos x + \frac{n-1}{n} \int{}{} \sin^{\,n-2}x \, dx \text{ and }\\ \int_{}{} \cos^{\,n} x \, dx \amp = \frac{1}{n} \cos^{n-1}x \sin x + \frac{n-1}{n} \int{}{} \cos^{\,n-2}x \, dx\text{.} \end{align*}
(a)

Determine the values of

\begin{equation*} \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \sin^{\,2} x \, dx \qquad \text{ and } \qquad \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \sin^{\,4} x \, dx\text{.} \end{equation*}
(b)

Use mathematical induction to prove that for each natural number \(n\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \sin^{\,2n}x \, dx \amp = \frac{1 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdots (2n - 1)}{2 \cdot 4 \cdot 6 \cdots (2n)} \frac{\pi}{2} \text{ and }\\ \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \sin^{\,2n+1}x \, dx \amp = \frac{2 \cdot 4 \cdot 6 \cdots (2n)}{1 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdots (2n + 1)}\text{.} \end{align*}

These are known as the Wallis sine formulas.

(c)

Use mathematical induction to prove that

\begin{align*} \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \cos^{\,2n}x \, dx \amp = \frac{1 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdots (2n - 1)}{2 \cdot 4 \cdot 6 \cdots (2n)} \frac{\pi}{2} \text{ and }\\ \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \cos^{\,2n+1}x \, dx \amp = \frac{2 \cdot 4 \cdot 6 \cdots (2n)}{1 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdots (2n+1)}\text{.} \end{align*}

These are known as the Wallis cosine formulas.
