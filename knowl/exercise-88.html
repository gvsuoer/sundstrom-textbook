Exercise 9.

An integer \(m\) is said to have the divides property provided that for all integers \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) if \(m\) divides \(ab\text{,}\) then \(m\) divides \(a\) or \(m\) divides \(b\text{.}\)

(a)

Using the symbols for quantifiers, write what it means to say that the integer \(m\) has the divides property.

(b)

Using the symbols for quantifiers, write what it means to say that the integer \(m\) does not have the divides property.

(c)

Write an English sentence stating what it means to say that the integer \(m\) does not have the divides property.
